WHAT CAUSES CLIMATE CHANGE?

Emissions Based on Greenhouse Gases

Based on the IPCC report in 2014, dangerous levels of greenhouse gases detected in the atmosphere is the main source of climate change, and carbon dioxide contributes to 65% of the total global greenhouse gas emissions.

Carbon dioxide emissions are usually human-induced through to deforestation by burning of forests, where the cleared land would be use for human purposes like agriculture, or burning of other organic materials like fossil fuels.

Greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide trap solar energy and keep the heat close to the Earth's surface instead of letting it escape into space.

The excessive atmospheric levels of carbon dioxide can lead to drastic temperature change, extreme weather events, destruction of wildlife habitat and populations and the rising of sea levels at an alarming rate.

Co2 Emissions per Capita by Region

North America tops the rank consistently for the past 5 decades as the region that emits the most carbon dioxide emissions per capita with an average of 18.91.

North America consistently tops the rank as their main source of energy for electricity, heat and transportation.
