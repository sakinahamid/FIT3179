WHAT CAUSES CLIMATE CHANGE?

Emissions Based on Greenhouse Gases

Based on the IPCC report in 2014, dangerous levels of greenhouse gases detected in the atmosphere is the main source of climate change, and carbon dioxide contributes to 65% of the total global greenhouse gas emissions.

Greenhouse gas emissions are caused by human activities like deforestation or burning of other organic materials like fossil fuels.

Greenhouse gases trap solar energy and keep the heat close to the Earth's surface instead of letting it escape into space.

The excessive levels of greenhouse gases can lead to drastic temperature change and the rising of sea levels at an alarming rate.

Co2 Emissions per Capita by Region

North America tops the rank consistently for the past 5 decades as the region that emits the most carbon dioxide emissions per capita with an average of 18.91.

North America consistently tops the rank as North American countries, like the United States, burn fossil fuels for electricity, heat and transportation.

The United States produced approximately 6,558 metric tons equivalent of CO2 emissions in 2019 alone.

WHAT ARE THE EFFECTS OF CLIMATE CHANGE?

CHANGE IN GLOBAL TEMPERATURE

Global Temperature Change (1961 - 2013)

The average global temperature on Earth has increased by around 1° Celsius since 1880. Two-thirds of the warming has occurred since 1975, at a rate of around 0.15-0.20°C per decade.

This temperature change has caused the melting of ice caps, especially in the North and South Pole (Antarctica) at an alarming rate. The Antarctica has lost 25,000km2 of ice shelf since the 1950s.

The siginifcant impacts of melting ice caps are the destruction of habitat for wildlife and marine life and the Earth losing the ability to deflect most of the solar rays from the Sun.

Global Temperature Change (1961 vs 2013)

The increase in temperature across the globe also brings along unpredictable weather patterns and phenomenons such as extreme drought and violent hurricanes.

This extreme weather would also cause significant damage such as reduced agricultural yields, health impacts in cities due to heat (e.g, skin cancer and heat stroke) and flooding and soil erosion around coastal areas.
